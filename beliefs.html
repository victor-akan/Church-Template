Our Foundation

What We Believe

Faith that is honest, rooted in Scripture, and alive in everyday life. Here is what shapes everything we do.

At Grace Community Church, our beliefs are not a list of rules — they are the living convictions that define how we see God, how we see people, and how we live in the world. We hold these beliefs with deep humility, knowing that faith is a journey and not a destination.

Statement of Faith

Our Core Beliefs

Grounded in historic Christian teaching. Lived out in practical love.

01
The Bible

We believe the Bible is the inspired Word of God — fully trustworthy, authoritative, and sufficient for all matters of faith and life. It is our map, our mirror, and our bread.

2 Timothy 3:16–17
02
God the Father

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three persons: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. God is holy, loving, just, and deeply personal — not a distant force but a knowing Father.

Deuteronomy 6:4 · Matthew 28:19
03
Jesus Christ

We believe Jesus is fully God and fully human. He was born of a virgin, lived a sinless life, died on the cross for our sins, rose bodily from the dead, and will return in glory.

John 1:1,14 · 1 Corinthians 15:3–4
04
The Holy Spirit

We believe the Holy Spirit dwells within every believer, bringing conviction, comfort, guidance, and the power to live a transformed life. He is active, personal, and present.

John 14:16–17 · Acts 1:8
05
Salvation by Grace

We believe every person is made in the image of God, yet all have sinned and need redemption. Salvation is a free gift — received through faith in Jesus Christ alone, not by our effort or merit.

Ephesians 2:8–9 · Romans 3:23
06
The Church

We believe the Church is the living body of Christ on earth — a community called to worship together, grow together, and serve the world. It is not a building; it is a people.

Ephesians 4:11–16 · 1 Corinthians 12:27
07
Baptism & Communion

We practice believer's baptism by immersion — a public declaration of faith. We also celebrate Communion regularly as a memorial of Christ's sacrifice and a renewal of our covenant with Him.

Matthew 28:19 · Luke 22:19
08
Eternity & Resurrection

We believe in the bodily resurrection of the dead and the return of Jesus Christ. Those who trust in Him will live with God forever. This hope shapes how we live today.

John 11:25 · Revelation 21:4
09
The Kingdom of God

We believe the Kingdom of God is already advancing — through acts of justice, healing, generosity, and love. We are called to be ambassadors of that Kingdom wherever we go.

Matthew 6:10 · Luke 4:18

"For I am not ashamed of the gospel, because it is the power of God
that brings salvation to everyone who believes."

Romans 1:16

Who We Are

Faith Without the Facade

We are not a perfect church — and we don't pretend to be. We are a community of imperfect people who have found grace, and want to extend that same grace to everyone who walks through our doors.

We welcome doubters, questioners, and seekers. We believe faith is honest — and that God is not afraid of your questions.

If you've been hurt by religion, disillusioned by the church, or simply curious about God — you are exactly who we want to sit with.

Isaiah 1:18

"My grace is sufficient for you, for my power is made perfect in weakness."

2 Corinthians 12:9

John 3:16

Good Questions

Things People Often Ask

Do I have to believe everything to attend?

Not at all. We welcome people at every stage of faith — or no faith at all. Come, explore, ask questions. Belief is a journey and you're welcome here while you're on yours.

What denomination are you?

We are an evangelical, non-denominational church. We hold to the historic Christian faith as summarized in the Apostles' Creed, while maintaining flexibility on secondary matters. What unites us is Jesus — not a label.

Is your church welcoming to people with a difficult past?

Absolutely. Some of our most committed members came to us in the middle of the hardest season of their lives. We believe grace is not a reward for good people — it's a rescue for all people.

Do you baptize children or only adults?

We practice believer's baptism — reserved for those who have personally made a decision to follow Christ. We hold a dedication service for infants and young children as a public commitment by parents to raise them in faith.

How do I learn more about what you believe?

Attending our Sunday services is the best way. You can also listen to our sermons online, or reach out directly — we'd love to sit with you over coffee and talk through any questions you have.

Next Steps

Questions? We'd Love to Talk.

Whether you're curious, skeptical, or just starting to explore — our pastors are here. No pressure. Just honest conversation.
